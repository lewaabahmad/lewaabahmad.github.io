Ego is the Enemy - Ryan Holiday
In reading this book, the following are questions I wanted to answer: 

1 - What does Ryan Holiday describe as ego? 

2 - What is an example of ego? 

3 - What characteristics are symptomatic of being consumed by ego? 

4 - Can ego be a positive force? 

5 - Who exemplifies the overcoming (and succumbing to) of ego? 

6 - How can I mitigate my own ego?

7 - What practical habits can I develop to drown my ego? 

8 - How am I being egotistic in my current life? 

Holiday breaks the book up into sections titled “Aspire”, “Success”, and “Failure.” The idea is that all circumstances fall into aspiring, succeeding, or failing. In itself, this is an interesting thought. Come to think of it, I am hard-pressed to find something that can not be grouped into those categories. Wallowing? Failure. Happy? Success. In awe? Success. Desire? Aspiration.

Within these sections, there are short chapters with anecdotes and examples of ego and how it plays out in one’s life. 

Holiday defines ego as “unhealthy belief in one’s own importance.” Simple enough. Rather than ego in psychological terms as popularized by Freud (the conscious observer.)
