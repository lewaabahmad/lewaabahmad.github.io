Authentic Being

An 8-Week Inquiry into Identity, Power, and Self-Authorship

Over the last decade I've spent a lot of time thinking about a simple question:

Why is it so hard for me to do what I say I want to do?

I don't mean that in the forcing myself to do menial work kind of way.

I am referring to how I want to be while I'm here. What I want to enable and create and embody in this life while I can. The true soul baring stuff. The art. The love. And yes, the playing, the kingdom building, and the sex and all the joys of life I can squeeze out of it.

This expedition I'm putting together is for people who are ready to lead an examined life.

You might think I'm talking self-improvement.

Fuck that.

I'm talking about being.

To BE - fully and honestly and compassionately. To have a deep capacity to sit confidently and firmly in the seat of self.

Look - I'm not a monk. I don't meditate everyday. I literally started this by saying that I have a hard time doing things I genuinely want to do sometimes.

What I do have is the capacity to intellectualize the fuck out of everything.

And it's through my inquiries that I've learned a lot spanning a great swath of history in the fields of philosophy and psychology.

But knowing isn't enough. It's a practice and concerted discipline to be embodied (to just be present in your body, not assuming a role or character) especially today.

To be an in-bodied soul in today's world is brave, hard, and more important than ever.

And so - you are invited. You are invited to join a small group of folk who are committed to exploring and examining their own lives. To define their own deeper existential truth and work to in-body it. The work is philosophical, psychological, and experiential.

The Core Inquiry

Authentic Being is an inquiry into the forces that shape a human life.

The work draws from traditions including:

  • Platonic philosophy and archetypal psychology
  • Buddhist insights into suffering and desire
  • Jungian shadow work
  • Internal Family Systems
  • somatic awareness and nervous system regulation
  • modern psychology of conditioning and habit formation

Most people do not lack intelligence, ambition, or discipline.

Today, more than ever, it is important to cultivate the capacity to hold tension.

The tension between:

  • who we are and who we could become
  • desire and discipline
  • freedom and responsibility
  • comfort and growth
  • impulse and integrity

When that tension becomes uncomfortable, most people escape it.

Through distraction. Through substances. Through fantasy. Through habits that relieve pressure in the moment while quietly weakening the person we are becoming.

Authentic embodiment is the ability to hold tension without collapsing into impulse.

The ability to remain present long enough to choose consciously. The ability to live deliberately rather than reactively.

We come together for self-authorship.

Enough poetics - here's the intended curriculum:

Week 1: Defining the Base Elements

  • What does it mean to be at all?
  • What does it mean to be a self and have an identity?
  • Platonic ideals (Theory of Forms)
  • Archetypes
  • Intersectionality and modern identity theory
  • Agency as a moral imperative

Goal: To map the lay of the land of being and place ourselves within it.

Week 2: Tension

  • The inherent relational nature of being
  • Buddhist "suffering" and escaping samsara
  • Tension between relational things
  • Stimulus as a response to feelings of tension

Goal: To understand our relational existence and identify our most consequential relations.

Week 3: Conditioning

  • Bio-reinforcement circuity and the science and psychology of conditioning
  • Conditioning as a byproduct of Stimulus
  • How have we been conditioned?
  • How can we influence our conditioning?
  • How can we condition ourselves?

Goal: To understand how we are conditioned on a day to day basis and become empowered to steward the direction of our own lives.

Week 4: Containment

  • Power as capacity to contain
  • Somatic awareness
  • The Eros of Life; and an inquiry into the erotic vs the pornographic
  • Cultivating containment and impulse control as a discipline

Goal: To define the capacity, practice, and discipline of holding tension, impulse control, and energetic transmutation.

Week 5: Shadow Integration

  • Internal Family Systems
  • Existential kink and the eroticism of self-degradation
  • Compassionate witnessing

Goal: To identify the conscious and unconscious internal forces influencing our lives and how to meet them compassionately for deeper, inner alignment.

Week 6: Detractors of Agency

  • Maladaptive coping
  • Addiction
  • Procrastination and avoidance
  • Comfort
  • Pattern interruption

Goal: To identify how we are meeting our needs in unproductive ways and create actionable cognitive behavioral therapy based practices to meet them healthily.

Week 7: The Morality of Power

  • Why the capacity for evil is necessary to truly be good
  • Trusting yourself
  • You are good (or, at the very least, redeemable)

Goal: To identify and eliminate barriers to personal power, self-love, and self-trust.

Week 8: Assuming the Seat of Self

  • What is the seat of self?
  • What does it mean to assume the seat of self and what does it feel like?

Goal: To ground inner alignment in a strong, rooted core sense of awareness and selfhood.

  • struggle with habits that undermine their self-respect
  • want discipline without becoming rigid or emotionally closed
  • are intellectually curious about philosophy and psychology
  • want deeper conversations than most containers provide
  • are ready to really examine being itself :)

This program is particularly valuable for folks who are:

  • professionals and creatives facing existential dissatisfaction
  • people navigating addiction or behavioral patterns
  • people seeking deeper alignment between values and action
